Hey!

I’m Zihe1 (or you can call me Zach2). I do computer engineering at the University of Cambridge. Most of my time is spent thinking about how frontier AI systems learn, Reinforcement Learning (RL), and addressing the practical issues of robustness and reliability in LLMs. This is my little corner of the Internet where I talk about what I’m currently obsessed with, cool projects I’ve worked on, stuff I’ve learnt, and some thoughts that have recently bubbled to the surface but refuse to pop.

About Me

I’m really curious about why RL works at all, and how the overoptimization problem could cause model outputs to become unintelligible to humans. Currently, I’m working on sample-efficient Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF). This summer I interned at Amazon Prime Video building a system to root-cause error logs at scale, using CatBoost and LLMs.

Previously, I did some research on developmental interpretability, where we investigated the high level learning dynamics of Natural Gradient Descent. I also interned at the Singapore AI Safety Institute, where I researched and benchmarked various hallucination detection methods. Last summer, I did the ARENA 4.0 program in London, where we built GPT-2 from scratch and implemented Proximal Policy Optimization to do RLHF. Before that, I deployed computer vision models for object detection and used topic modelling analysis to automate categorization of web feedback at the Changi Airport Group ML team.

Singapore is where I call home, and before university I served 2 years in the Republic of Singapore Air Force as a security trooper. Outside work, you’ll probably find me dancing to hip-hop and R&B music, or rewatching old Wong Kar Wai films.
