The Discrete Fourier Transform and Fast Fourier Transform

Published on March 28, 2025

We analyze 1 the theoretical properties of both the Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) and the optimized Fast Fourier Transform (FFT), and estimate their algorithmic complexity. All code available on Github. Slides available here. Discrete Fourier Transform Theory The DFT converts a finite-length time-domain signal into its frequency-domain representation. For an input signal $x[n]$ of length $N$, where $0 \le n \le N-1$, the DFT is defined as: $$ X[k] = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x[n]\, W_N^{nk}, \quad 0 \le k \le N-1 \label{eq:DFT} $$ where $W_N=e^{-j\frac{2\pi}{N}}$ is the $N$-th principal root of unity, and $X[k]$ is component at frequency $kf_s/N$ with sampling frequency $f_s$.
